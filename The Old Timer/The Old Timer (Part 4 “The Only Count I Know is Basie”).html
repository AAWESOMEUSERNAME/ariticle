The Old Timer (Part 4: “The Only Count I Know is Basie”)
旧时代舞者（第四部分：“我唯一知道的计拍法叫贝西”）
MARCH 30, 2010
3月 30, 2010
Musicality in the Old Timer world is an enormous part of why they danced the way they did. It’s a topic I’m infatuated with, and these conclusions may not last long as I keep thinking, or as I discuss this topic with others. But I hope you will find something interesting or inspiring in them. In our last installment, music began to enter into our equation when we mentioned how different a night of dancing would have been to a dancer who would have heard the same style of swing dance music played almost every night of a year. For this installment, we dive a lot deeper into the Old Timer dancers and how music effected their dancing.
在根源舞者的世界里，音乐性对他们的跳舞方式影响巨大。这是一个我很着迷的话题，接下来说的这些结论，可能会在我的反复思考或与别人的讨论后发生变化。但是我希望你能从中获得启发和乐趣。在上一个部分，音乐开始进入我们的反应方程式，我们提到如果一个舞者一整年几乎每晚听到的音乐都是同样风格会产生多大的不同。这个部分，我们会进一步深挖音乐对根源舞者的影响。
The Only Count I know is Basie
“我唯一知道的计拍法叫贝西”
Imagine you’re a dancer in the 1930s. Dancing for you means going out at several nights a week, and every night to a different big band, each one using different arrangements. When the leader announces he’s going to play “Flying Home,” you don’t know anything about how the song is going to sound except that the melody will roughly go “Bad-da-daaa, da-da-dadadum…Bad-da-daaa, da-da-dadadum…etc.”
如果你是一个1930年代的舞者，跳舞意味着每周出去好几次，每次都跟着不同的大乐队跳舞，而不同的乐队有不同的编排。当乐队领班说接下来演奏“flying home”时，你根本不知道接下来这首歌听起来什么样，唯一确定的就是旋律大概是“Bad-da-daaa, da-da-dadadum…Bad-da-daaa,da-da-dadadum…这样。”
Any of the intricate stuff in this night’s arrangement, or the improvised notes a soloist adds to the music make listening to it enjoyable and rewarding, but the chances of you as a dancer being able to change your dancing specifically to all those tiny sections as they fly by your ears are slim, especially because they would take such mental effort and skill that it’d probably be too mentally frustrating to even try it. The result–you’d spend more time dancing musically to the overall phrasing or big-picture melody, the overall flow of the music. It’s the “flying home” melody, after all. And if you don’t get it the first few times, they’ll repeat it. Sure, the arranger might switch it up a bit, tweak it a little, but you can work with that a lot easier than trying to be incredibly musical on every small thing that passes by. You can hit things in the small part of the music, but it’s through subtle, small changes in your overall movements–things that are easier to manipulate in the moment.
这些复杂的编排或者独奏者的即兴会使当晚的音乐十分令人享受，但是舞者几乎不可能用舞蹈去一一回应那些一闪而过的音乐细节，这种回应是很费脑子的，哪怕只是试一试，都可能让你头疼到自闭。结果就是，舞者开始花更多的时间去跟着整体的乐句结构，整体旋律，整个音乐的流动感去表达自己的音乐性。毕竟这是“flying home”的旋律，即使最初几次你可能反应不过来，他们还会再演奏的。尽管作曲者可能对原曲进行一些改动，但是相较于所有音乐细节都去表现，依然要简单不少。你可以去回应那些音乐细节，但是这种回应都是穿插在整体舞蹈中的小变换，一些更容易当即作出反应的小动作。
Now, put yourself in your modern dance shoes. When you go out dancing, you will hear the Fat’s Waller recording of “All that meat and no potatoes,” which you have heard fifty seven times this year alone. (For me, that’s fine; it’s a good song.) You know every note of that song, sub consciously if not consciously. When you dance to it, you will probably be able to hit and emphasize all the minor musical intricacies very well.
现在回到你现代舞者的立场。当你出去跳舞的时候，一定会听到Fat’s Waller的《All that meat and no potatoes》，一首你仅这一年就已经听了57次的曲子（对我来说这没什么，这歌挺好的。）你清楚的知道这首歌的每一个细节，即使不是有意识的记住，也已经在潜意识里留下印记。当你跳这首曲子时，可能有能力去很好的击中或强调所有细微的复杂音乐性。
Now, here’s where it gets strange: When you suddenly hear a song you don’t know, you might still try to dance to the musical intricacies in it. If the song isn’t full of obvious swing music cliches that will make that easy, you will have a rough flow, or make some mistakes and end up looking really un-musical, and walk away from the dance not satisfied with the musicality in your dance.
接下来要发生的事情就有点奇怪了：当你突然听到一首不知道的曲子，可能还会想去表现那些复杂的音乐性。如果那首歌不是简单的千篇一律的流行摇摆乐，你可能会跳的很僵硬，或者犯很多错误，结果看起来跳的十分没有音乐性，最后在挫败中黯然离场。
You have become a micro-musicality dancer. You have trained your musicality muscles to mainly focus on the small stuff, perhaps because you have equated “musicality” to mean “being able to dance to a song’s musical changes very intricately.” If you are a follow, this is not a problem, but a strength–it’s the only way for you to play a major roll in musicality, since the leader interprets all the bigger movements (More on this later.) If you’re a leader and this describes you, then you have forgotten how to take pleasure and satisfaction in your overall “big-picture” musicality. This is a very large difference between the old time dancer and the new.
这时，你已经变成了一个微观音乐性舞者。或许是由于你把“音乐性”等价的理解为“有能力去跟着音乐中复杂的变化跳舞”,你的音乐性已经被训练得主要关注细小的东西。如果你是一个follower，这不是个大问题，反而是优势——这是你仅有的可以主导的音乐性表达方式，因为leader掌控着所有大的移动（之后再详谈。）如果你是一个这样的leader，那么你已经忘记了如何在大的音乐性中寻找乐趣和满足。这是根源舞者和当代舞者一个很大的差异。
I began thinking about this topic after talking to Nick Williams and David Rehm, who had the topic as a part of a musicality class they put on together. They told me how they had noticed the old time dancers phrased1 for their musicality, whereas a lot of new dancers seem to put more emphasis on what we call micro-musicality; the minute musical stuff. They deserve credit for planting the seed that began my own explorations into old-timer musicality. The more I thought about it, the more obvious sense it made, and the more I could formulate why it was that way for an old timer dancer. This was when I composed the opening paragraph, a little speech I give in classes on musicality. This musicality is a very important aspect to why classic dancing looks the way it does.
和Nick Williams、David Rehm交谈了一次促使我开始思考这个话题，这部分东西也被放在了他们关于音乐性的课程里。他们和我分享了，他们是如何发现根源舞者们表达音乐性的方式是“造句”1，而不是像现代舞者那样似乎更在意所谓的微观音乐性——那些转瞬即逝的东西。是他们在我心里埋下了探索老舞者音乐性的种子。随着我的不断思考，这种感觉越来越明显，我也得以逐渐理解根源舞者们以那种方式跳舞的原因。这是为我在音乐性系列课程上一小段开场演讲做准备时想到的东西。这种音乐性是探索根源舞者跳舞方式的一个重要切面。
For instance, look at Hellzapoppin, which was originally choreographed to “Jumpin’ at the Woodside,” but because it was danced to overall phrases, fits nicely into other songs. Look at the California, a piece of choreography that is universal to almost any AABA song. Look at Al Minns dancing to Jumpin at the Woodside at 1:15 in this clip. Since the old timer did most of their social dancing to live music, and thus their musicality was refined to live music, I think an old time dancer evolves their movements roughly in proportion to the importance of things in the music. Overall pulse and the way they step is determined by the songs feeling and mood. The songs melody and phrasing commands their big movements, like turns and patterns. The small stuff in the music gets a proportionately small amount of attention.
由于根源舞者们大部分时候都跟着现场乐队跳舞，他们的音乐性得以被现场音乐有效训练，我想他们的动作大体上是根据音乐中一些重要的东西演化而来。整体的律动以及踩步子的方式由曲子的感觉和情绪决定。大的动作，如转圈或固定连招，则被曲子的旋律和结构支配。音乐中细碎的细节则相应的不会被关注太多。
I’m not suggesting that modern dancers should give up their micro-musicality–it’s a great tool, show’s great skill, and makes it fun to dance to certain songs repeatedly; it’s a natural product of the DJ dance world. In a way, it’s something important we have contributed to the dance, like advanced frame and comfortable lead/follow skills. But I do think we should (1) remember phrasing is an important, powerful, and subtle musicality skill, and that (2) we need to always keep our micro-musicality in proportion, lest it dominate our dancing.
我不是在建议现代舞者放弃自己的微观音乐性——那是一种很好的表现技术的工具，并且使跳重复的曲子更有乐趣，这是DJ舞蹈世界自然的产物。某种程度上，这是我们对这种舞蹈的重要贡献，同样的还有“框架”以及舒适的领舞跟舞技巧。但是我确实认为我们应该（1）意识到结构性是一种重要的，强有力的，精妙的音乐性技巧。（2）我们需要把自己的微观音乐性控制在一个合适的比例，以免它主导我们的舞蹈。
Where micro-musicality probably did come into play with old timers was jazz tropes–Swing music can be pretty predictable, and so I think a lot of micro-musicality evolved out of those predictable jazz moments. For instance, we all know that if we hear “Shave and a haircut,” we can almost guarantee a “TWO BITS!” will follow. Fred Astaire and Ginger Rogers make constant use of the swing tropes in “Follow the Fleet,” where the bugle call rag trumpet always prompts them to tap-out the final part, no matter what they’re doing at the time. Jazz tropes were so common that band leaders like Artie Shaw began setting up Jazz tropes only to twist their endings to psych-out listeners (In Artie’s case, often specifically to piss off dancers, whom he despised).2
但是，根源舞者也会在jazz tropes（译者注：鸽子并没有找到能与之对应的中文概念，此处可简单理解为一些约定俗成的音乐小段落。）中玩一些微观音乐性——摇摆乐有时是十分容易预测的，所以我认为很多微观音乐性正是演变自这种很容易预测的瞬间。举个例子，我们都知道如果听到“Shave and a haircut” （此处应有一段音频todo） 我们基本可以确定后面会紧跟着“那两拍”。Fred Astaire和Ginger Rogers就在电影《海上恋舞（Follow the Fleet）》中频繁的使用swing tropes，无论他们正在做什么，标志性的军号总能推动他们用脚打出最后的那部分拍子。jazz tropes十分普遍，以至于像Artie Shaw这样的乐队领班会通过jazz tropes来改变他们曲子的结尾以图干扰听众（在Artie Shaw这里，通常是刻意驱赶他讨厌的舞者们）。2
For leaders, I think this is why old timer’s tend to have a few fancy moves to bust out when people are watching, but otherwise dance a whole bunch of basic movements that they alter slightly with footwork or feel. In most of the social dancing we have, like Bobby McGees, or even the Spirit Moves jams, or even Frankie Manning, a master of innovative moves (http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e00F7OimG6U), we see leaders dominantly keeping to basic moves and playing within those moves.
对于leader来说，我想这就是为什么根源舞者会在别人关注的时候跳一些华丽的舞步炸场，但是其他时候都是跳一些基本的动作并稍微改变下步子或者感觉。在我们能找到的大部分社交舞视频，像Bobby McGees，或在Spirit Moves的jams里，甚至是舞步发明大师Frankie Manning，都会看到leader主要是在做一些基本的动作然后在这些动作中玩变化。 （-todo视频链接-）
Here’s the great news, thanks to in-depth thinking and research done on musicality by my partner and a classically-trained musician, Kate Hedin: Micro-musicality has liberated the swing dance follower. Since they have almost no control over the lead moves of the dance, and more-or-less have to match their partners for pulse and flow, followers in history have not had a chance to do much more than generic “this is the mood of the song” musicality and throwing in small, odd stylings–there is very little instances of follower-specific musicality in old timer footage. But while a follower has no control over the lead moves, she can hit the small stuff. Great modern day followers are capable of expressing what they feel to the music very specifically.
接下来是好消息。经过对音乐性的深入研究和思考，我的伙伴，经过系统训练的音乐家，Kate Hedin提出：微观音乐性解放了摇摆舞中的跟舞。由于他们基本对舞步没有控制权，并且或多或少需要匹配对方的律动和流动，历史上的follower们从来没有机会做出超过一般如“这是这首歌的情绪”的音乐性表达并开创出小众风格——根源舞者的步子基本没有follower专属的音乐性表达的例子。虽然follower们无法控制整体舞步，她们可以去表达小的细节。当代杰出的follower们在精确表达音乐中的自我感受上极富才华。
The way the old timers thought of phrasing points to a broader point of how they experienced music differently than we tend to. Norma Miller used to say, famously “The only count I know is Basie.”
根源舞者们对音乐结构划分的方式引出了另一个广泛的观点：他们感受音乐的方式和我们倾向的方式有所不同。Norma Miller曾经有一句名言:“我唯一知道的计拍法叫贝西。”（译者注：Count Basie，著名爵士音乐家，count可译作节拍，此处引申为计拍法，一语双关。）
The Count Basie pun is easy to say (and nod your head at, soulfully and knowingly) but hard for some dancers, especially new ones or musically trained ones, to fully understand right away. It’s incredibly common for someone in a beginner class to ask “Why is a turn 6-counts–doesn’t that mess things up, musically?”3 What Norma means, to be very technical about it, is that the original dancers had no training in counts, and didn’t assume any rules about moves taking 4 or 8 counts to do so that they fit perfectly into the structure of swing music. (Besides, anyone who sees strict 8-count dancing can see how repetitive and predictable it looks after a few moments, something you don’t want swing dancing to look or feel like).
这一处Count Basie的双关说起来容易（并且让人会心一笑），但是对很多舞者，特别是新舞者或者受过音乐训练的人来说，是很难马上完全理解的。在新人课上有一个频繁到令人无法置信的问题：“为什么一个6拍的转圈不会打乱整体音乐性？”3从技术的角度来讲，Norma的意思是根源舞者们从来没有受过查拍子的训练，也不会预设任何步子应该是4拍或者8拍的以适配整个音乐结构。（并且，任何看过一点儿严格的8拍舞蹈的人都知道那是何等的机械，你不会想要摇摆舞是那个样子。）
Today, we summarize it by saying the original dancers did a lot of 8-count moves, because they are musical, but when it got down to it, only really cared about 2-count chunks, or up-beats and down beats. Those two-beat chunks are the basic Legos of swing music, and a dancer just puts those Legos together. That’s why you can break almost all moves easily into 2-beat chunks. (For instance, a side pass– a rock-step two-beat chunk, a triple step two-beat chunk, and another triple step two-beat chunk.)
今天我们总结到根源舞者有很多8拍的步子，因为他们跳的很有音乐性，但是仔细考究就会发现，他们在意的只有2拍的小段，或者轻重拍。这些2拍的小段是摇摆乐的“基础乐高积木”，舞者做的就是把这些积木拼起来。这就是为什么你可以很容易把所有动作分解成2拍的组合。（比如，一个交换位置动作 —— 一个rock-step 2拍，一个triple step 2拍，再一个triple step 2拍。）
The pros to not concerning yourself with counts is that as a dancer, you can spend more time paying attention to the music, and allowing your movements to be driven by it naturally rather than specific dance steps set to pre-planned times. The cons to not thinking about counts is the simple fact that understanding counts are a great way to learn dance and a great way to control and hone rhythm and keep it from getting messy, which a lot of dancers who don’t pay attention to counts have problems with.
作为一个舞者不太执着于拍子的好处是，你可以把更多的注意力放在音乐上，让自己的动作自然的被音乐驱动，而不是跳预先设想的特定动作。相应的坏处是，不在意拍子的舞者要面对的无法维持稳定节奏的困扰。理清楚拍子是一种很好的学习跳舞的方式，也是一个很好的保持稳定节奏不被打乱的方式。

1. I’d like to explain why I have chosen to use the word “phrasing.” The old timers (for the most part) didn’t think of their music in technical terms, and phrasing is most definitely a technical word, so in that sense, it doesn’t seem like it’s the perfect choice. But as a term, a “phrase” of jazz music describes nicely the small musical stories that are told in a swing song. It describes the build-up and climax of a melody which are precisely the parts of music a good dancer would have been paying attention to. It is for this reason that I think “phrasing” is perhaps the best one-word way of describing their sense of big-picture musicality.
1. 我需要解释一下我为什么会使用“造句”这个词。（译者注：类似于中文的一词多义，phrase可做名词“乐句”， 也可做动词“造句”。）大部分根源舞者不使用技术概念去思考他们的音乐，“乐句”肯定是一个技术词汇，这个角度看，好像不是一个好的选择。但是作为一个术语，一个“乐句”很好的形容了摇摆乐中讲述的小音乐故事。这个词形容了一段旋律的开始和高潮，这恰恰是一个好的舞者会在音乐中关注的部分。这就是为什么我觉得“造句”可能是用以描述他们大图景音乐性的最佳词语。
But, to be more specific, the big picture parts of the music that would have been the priority of the old timers also include the mood of the music (energetic phrases and drum solos compared to mellow phrases and piano solos) and the melodic narrative as a whole. If at any point in this discussion I get too white, just let me know.
但是，更准确的说，根源舞者们首要关注的音乐的大图景同样包括音乐的情绪（比如富有活力的乐句和鼓独奏就不同于柔和的乐句和钢琴独奏）以及旋律性叙事。如果本篇讨论中的任何观点过于“白人”化，请让我知道。
2. Artie Shaw was an infamous grouch. Among one of the many things he disliked was dancers, whom he thought would dance to “a windshield wiper” as long as it had a steady beat. It is apparently because of this dislike that his soloist would break the form of jazz tropes, and his drummers would often hit odd beats to accentuate the music. Perhaps to Artie’s corpse’s chagrin, the result was a group of songs that became a great tool for swing choreographers like Kevin and Carla in the early 2000s, who found Artie’s songs so interesting that they inspired original and interesting choreography.
2. Artie Shaw 是出了名的性情古怪。众多他讨厌的事物之一便是舞者，他认为，即使是个“雨刷器”，只要有稳定的节拍，就会引来舞者跳舞。显然是因为这种厌恶，他的独奏经常会打破jazz tropes，他的鼓手经常打奇数拍来恶心舞者。结果这些曲子成了如2000年代早期Kevin和Carla这样编舞者的极好工具，他们发现Artie的曲子十分有趣，并激发出了很多有意思的原创编舞——Artie听了估计要从棺材里跳出来。
So, imagine Artie Shaw being asked to make a “soundie,” the 1930s equivalent of a music video. Many of them had dancing as part of their production, but Artie Shaw refused having dancers being filmed while his musicians were playing and should get all the attention. But, Artie Shaw was a favorite dance band of the era, and its understandable the producers would want to market to that audience. In the soundie for ” Lady Be Good,” the producers found their loophole: by filming collegiate shag dancers separately on a different sound stage on a different day, they faded the dancing shots in and out over the video for the band.
所以，你就可以相见当Artie Shaw被要求拍一个小的展示1930年代的音乐短片是一副怎样的场景了。很多这种短片都会把舞者作为内容的一部分，但是Artie Shaw拒绝在自己的乐手们演奏的时候有舞者出现，认为乐手应该吸引所有的注意。但是，Artie Shaw的乐队在那个年代很受舞者欢迎，这也是为什么制片人希望打入舞者们的市场。在《Lady Be Good》的短片中，制片人钻了个空子：在另外的一天拍一群Collegiate shag 舞者跟着不同的音乐跳舞，然后在Artie乐队的片子里不断穿插混入舞者的视频。
3. My own answer for why a turn can take 6-beats: It’s the perfect amount of time for one turn to take. Four counts is too fast for every turn. Eight counts is too long. Six is just right.
